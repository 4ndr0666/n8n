Getting started with CRM automation: Essential guide & templates included

Discover how to streamline your sales & marketing with our CRM automation guide. Access customizable templates to jumpstart your automation process!

Federico Trotta
August 8, 2024 ∙ 14 minutes read

A CRM can be a fundamental tool for managing and closing deals, but it does its job only if the data is accurate and up-to-date at any moment. Regular updates are necessary to keep track of ongoing deals, correspondences, and team tasks.

That’s where CRM automation comes in.

Read on to discover what CRM automation is, why and when to use it for your business, the integrations modern CRM tools offer, and how to easily automate and customize CRM workflows with n8n.

Let's dive in!

What is CRM automation?

CRM automation refers to the use of technology to streamline and automate various tasks and processes within a Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system. It aims to reduce manual work, enhance efficiency, and ensure consistency in how customer data and interactions are managed.

Here's what CRM automation typically involves:

  1. Automating routine tasks: CRM automation can handle repetitive tasks like data entry, updating records, and managing customer information. For instance, when a new lead is entered into the system, automation can ensure that all relevant details are recorded without manual input.
  2. Streamlining sales processes: Automation can help manage ongoing deals by automatically updating their statuses, scheduling follow-ups, and sending reminders. This ensures that sales teams can focus on closing deals rather than managing administrative tasks.
  3. Enhancing communication: Automated workflows can be set up to send personalized emails, messages, or notifications based on specific triggers, such as a customer reaching a particular stage in their journey or a deal moving forward.
  4. Automating lead management: CRM automation helps in tracking and nurturing leads by assigning them to the right salespeople, sending follow-up communications, and monitoring their progress through the sales funnel.
  5. Tracking interactions across channels: CRM systems can automatically log and track customer interactions across multiple channels, such as emails, phone calls, social media, and website visits. This creates a unified view of each customer’s journey.
  6. Reporting and analytics: Automation can generate reports and insights without manual intervention, helping teams analyze performance, track KPIs, and make data-driven decisions.

By leveraging CRM automation, businesses can improve productivity, reduce errors, and provide a more seamless and personalized customer experience.

What are some of the benefits of CRM automation?

Workflow automation improves the capabilities of a CRM system by offering numerous benefits that streamline operations, improve customer relations, and drive business growth.

Here's a list of the key benefits of automating CRM workflows, particularly useful in the case of large enterprises:

  • Increased efficiency: Automation eliminates manual data entry and routine tasks, reducing the time and effort required for administrative duties. This allows teams to focus on high-value activities, improving productivity.
  • Improved data accuracy: Automated processes minimize the risk of human error in data entry and updates, ensuring that customer information is accurate and up-to-date. This leads to reliable and trusty data.
  • Scalability: For large enterprises, managing customer relationships manually becomes increasingly challenging. Automation, instead, allows CRMs to scale effortlessly, handling larger volumes of data and interactions without additional strain on resources.
  • Personalized communication: Automated workflows enable the delivery of personalized messages based on customer behaviors and preferences. This targeted approach enhances customer engagement and satisfaction, as communications have to be more relevant and timely today.
  • Enhanced lead management: Automation helps in capturing, tracking, and nurturing leads more effectively. By automating lead scoring and assignment, sales teams can prioritize high-potential prospects and focus their efforts on closing deals, thus increasing conversion rates.

What CRM has the best native automation?

The "best" CRM for automation can depend on various factors, such as your business size, industry, specific needs, and budget. Here are some highly regarded CRMs known for their automation capabilities:

Zoho CMR automation

Zoho is a sales CRM software that manages sales, marketing, and support. It offers everything you need to increase leads, accelerate sales, and measure sales performance.

Key Features:

  • Workflow automation: Zoho automates routine tasks such as sending emails, updating fields, and creating tasks based on predefined triggers and rules.
  • Blueprint: It ensures process consistency by defining and automating every step of a sales process, guiding teams through each stage.
  • Zia AI: It’s an AI-powered assistant that automates lead scoring, predictive sales insights, and anomaly detection to optimize sales efforts.

Pros:

  • Trial plans: Every license presents the possibility for a test with a free trial.
  • Intuitively: Its interface is immediately intuitive. The software is easy to learn and use.
  • Lots of integrations: Zoho CRM integrates with other Zoho and other 3rd party applications, providing businesses with an all-in-one solution for their operations.

Cons:

  • Learning curve: While Zoho CRM is easy to use, it requires some learning, which could be a challenge for businesses with limited resources or technical knowledge.
  • Technical complexity: While it is generally considered to be user-friendly, it has a certain level of technical complexity that may not be suitable for businesses with limited technical expertise.
  • Updating and fetching records at scale: When dealing with large amounts of data, Zoho CRM may struggle to update and fetch records efficiently. This can cause delays and hinder the performance of the CRM system, particularly for businesses with vast customer bases or complex data sets.

Pricing:

  • A professional  license to improve customer acquisition and accelerate growth - 39$/Mo (with a free trial);
  • A premium license to manage global CX operations with the complete CRM - 79$/Mo (with a free trial);
  • Contact sales for the custom license.

Limitations of CRMs native automation builder

  • High costs: Access to the most valuable automation features is often restricted to premium pricing tiers, making them costly for many businesses.
  • Limited flexibility: Native automation tools usually offer less flexibility, confining users to predefined workflows that might not suit all needs.
  • Fewer features: Compared to workflow automation platforms like n8n, CRM-native automation typically provides fewer features, lacking advanced AI capabilities, extensive customization, and a broad range of integrations (over 100 in the case of n8n).
  • Restricted integrations: Native CRM automation often supports fewer integrations, limiting the ability to connect with other tools and systems seamlessly.

For businesses seeking comprehensive and adaptable automation solutions, these limitations can be significant, leading them to explore more versatile platforms.

Qualify new leads in Google Sheets with AI

We know that a lot of companies use Google Sheets as databases and this is why we created an automation for this particular case:

Here’s what this automation does:

  1. It is triggered when a new row is added to a Google Sheet of your choice.
  2. The OpenAI node processes the lead’s information. To do so, the system query contains detailed qualification rules and the response format.

How do I get started with CRM automation in n8n?

As you’ve already checked some practical CRM automation examples, how about diving deeper into automating your first CRM workflow with n8n?

The workflow, we’ll be looking at, reads every reply received from a cold email campaign and determines whether or not the lead is interested in a meeting. If the lead is interested, a deal will be made with Pipedrive.

Requirements

To use this template, you need:

  • At least one active Gmail account;
  • An active Pipedrive account with a valid API token;
  • An active OpenAI account with a valid API key.

Step 1: Credentials setup

When you use the template for the first time, you have to complete the setup that consists of the following:

  • Adding your Gmail account credentials;
  • Adding your Pipedrive API token;
  • Adding your OpenAI API key.

Step 2: Configure your Gmail account

This template has two Gmail triggers.

If you use only one Gmail account, delete one of the two nodes.

If you use more than two, duplicate a Gmail node as many times as you need, connect them to the Get email node, insert your credentials in the Credential to connect with field, and you’re ready to use the triggers.


Step 3: Configure Pipedrive

In Pipedrive, click on your credentials. Then, go to company settings > Data fields > Person and click on add custom field; call it in_campaign.
In the n8n’s template, the blocks Search Person in CRM and Get Person from CRM are already set up to search for and get persons in Pipedrive.

Step 4: Configure the OpenAI integration

The Is interested? block manages the integration with OpenAI.

The most important field here is Content.

Here you have to:

  • Write the message that will be delivered to the people retrieved in Pipedrive to understand if they qualify as interested leads. The block already provides a predefined message, but you can customize it to fulfill your needs. A message template idea could be the following:
    “As a top-notch [describe the role of the lead], you excel at [describe the result the lead provides]. We understand that your role involves [describe the goal reached by the lead], so we’d love to learn more about your approach and share some insights that could help us both improve our strategies. Could we schedule a virtual coffee sometime soon?

Looking forward to connecting with you!”

  • The second part of the predefined content provides a structure that must be followed to make the workflow work correctly as it retrieves the email of the user and asks if they are interested in a meeting. You can leave it as is to be sure to make no mistake.

Step 5: Get the response and make the deal

The last three blocks of the CRM automation workflow make the following:

  • Get response. This Code node block gets the response of the user.
  • If interested. This If node block defines whether the response qualifies the user as interested in a meeting or not. Create deal in CRM. This node block creates a deal in Pipedrive for the leads interested in a meeting. Here make sure that the field Resource is set on Deal and the field  Operation is set on Create.

And... here you go! You’re ready to launch your CRM marketing automation workflow!

Frequently asked questions about CRM automation

What is the difference between CRM and sales automation?

CRM encompasses managing customer relationships throughout their lifecycle, including data analysis and customer interaction tracking. Sales automation specifically automates sales-related tasks like email campaigns and scheduling to streamline and optimize sales processes, focusing on efficiency rather than holistic relationship management.

What is the difference between CRM and marketing automation?

CRM automation offers tools for sales tracking, customer service, and interaction management. Marketing automation, instead, provides campaign management, email marketing, and lead development capabilities.

Is CRM automation only for enterprise-level organizations?

Absolutely not. Just like CRM platforms, automation is highly scalable. Many automation tools are built to support your needs at any level. Straight out of the box, they can grow alongside a small business or scale up to meet the demands of an enterprise organization.
